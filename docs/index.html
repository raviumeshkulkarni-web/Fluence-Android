The Problem

The Dictation Bottleneck

Traditional dictation systems are either slow, unpunctuated, or restricted to a specific custom keyboard. Google's streaming STT dictates word-by-word and frequently gets homophones wrong because it lacks contextual sentence-level comprehension. Furthermore, closed-source keyboards pose a massive telemetry and data privacy risk.

The Solution

The Fluence Approach

Fluence waits for you to finish your sentence/phrase, transcribes it using OpenAI's Whisper Large v3 model on Groq's LPU inference hardware in less than a second, and automatically injects fully-punctuated, context-aware text directly at your cursor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Fluence free to use?

Yes, Fluence is 100% free and open-source under the Apache 2.0 license. Since transcription is processed using API keys, you only pay for your own usage. Groq offers a generous free tier for developers, and even on paid tiers, Whisper transcription costs a fraction of a cent per hour of usage.

How is my data privacy protected?

Fluence is built on ironclad privacy principles. There is zero telemetry and no server logging. Audio recording is ephemeral—sent securely to the Whisper API, transcribed, and instantly discarded. Your Groq API key is stored safely on your device inside the Android Keystore using hardware-backed encryption.

Does it support languages other than English?

Yes. Whisper Large v3 supports 99+ languages with high accuracy. It features automatic language detection (so you can speak in your native language, and it will transcribe correctly) and automatic translation to English if configured.

Can it run completely offline?

Yes! Fluence now supports 100% offline, on-device voice dictation. By toggling "Offline Transcription" in the settings, the app downloads an optimized on-device SenseVoice-Small ONNX model (~230MB). Once downloaded, all transcription is executed completely locally on your device with zero internet connectivity and zero data egress, providing maximum privacy and zero latency.
